Whether you prefer museums or the theater, zoos or gardens, sports entertainment or shopping excursions, there are so many things to do in New York City, there is something to do for everyone! New York is a vibrant and eclectic place. There is an incredibly diverse range of things to see and do, but there are a few attractions like no other!

  • Empire State Building: New York's tallest building, the Empire State Building is an Art Deco masterpiece that opened in 1931. Tourists in Manhattan can take the elevator up to the 86th-floor observatory deck and view the streets of Manhattan from nearly 1,500 feet above street level! The Empire State Building is a must-see New York attraction in Manhattan.

  • United Nations Building: Tours of the United Nations Building give NYC tourists a behind-the-scenes look at one of New York's most important buildings. See where the leaders of the world meet to discuss humanitarian issues, and learn about the storied history of this New York institution when you visit the U.N. Building in NYC.

  • South Street Seaport: One of the most beautiful areas in NYC, the South St. Seaport in New York's Financial District lies on the gorgeous NYC harbor. Enjoy great shopping in NYC, plenty of top New York restaurants, and some of the best NYC nightlife at the South St. Seaport in Manhattan.

  • Museum of Modern Art: The most prestigious NYC museum and art gallery, the Museum of Modern Art is home to some of the most famous paintings of all time. Get an up close look at classic paintings like Van Gogh's Starry Night and Salvador Dali's The Persistence of Memory as well as contemporary pieces from Andy Warhol at MoMA New York City.

  • Central Park: The biggest and most beautiful park in New York City is Central Park NY. Spanning 843 acres, and offering tons of things to do in NYC for tourists - including the famous Central Park Carousel and the Wollman Ice Skating Rink - Central Park is the perfect way to take a break from the hustle and bustle of New York City.

  • Brooklyn Bridge: Completed in 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge is one of the oldest and longest suspension bridges in the United States. This iconic bridge, a National Historic Landmark since 1964, spans the East River and connects the New York City boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn.

  • Grand Central Station: With 44 platforms and 67 tracks along them, Grand Central Terminal, also known as Grand Central Station, is the longest train station in the world by number of platforms, and one of the most iconic sights in New York City. This magnificent terminal has been featured in countless films, including The Bone Collector, I Am Legend, Men in Black, and North by Northwest.

  • Metropolitan Museum of Art: Located on Manhattan's Museum Mile, just steps from Central Park and Fifth Avenue shopping, the Metropolitan Museum of Art is a massive fine arts museum stretching a quarter-mile and occupying more than 2,000,000 square feet. Housed within the museum are classic works of art, including Hokusai's The Great Wave off Kanagawa, Duccio's Madonna and Child, Jackson Pollock's Autumn Rhythm (Number 30), and Picasso's portrait of Gertrude Stein.

  • Madison Square Garden: Madison Square Garden is a massive, multi-purpose indoor arena located atop Manhattan's Pennsylvania Station. Having opened on February 11, 1968, the arena, often referred to simply as "MSG" or "The Garden," is the premier music venue in NYC, the oldest arena in the NHL, and the second-oldest arena in the NBA. It is also home to the Theater at Madison Square Garden, a 5,600-seat concert venue. (NYCTourist.com Reference)
